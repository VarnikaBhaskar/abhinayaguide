NAVARASA


The word Navarasa is derived from the Sanskrit words nava, meaning nine, and rasa, meaning aesthetic experience.

Shringaara Haasya Karuna Roudra Veera Bhayaanakaah
Beebhatsaadbhuta Saantashcha nava naatye Rasaa smritaah

Navarasa in Bharathanatyam refers to the nine fundamental emotions conveyed through Abhinaya.

Shringaara

Love


Represents beauty, attraction, romance, and devotion.


Haasya

Humour and laughter


Expresses joy, amusement, playfulness.


Karuna

Compassion


Expresses sorrow, empathy and emotional sensitivity.


Roudra

Anger


Expresses fury, rage and power.


Veera

Heroism


Expresses bravery, confidence and pride.


Bhaya

Fear


Expresses fear, anxiety and nervousness.


Beebhatsa

Disgust


Expresses aversion, revulsion and discomfort.


Adbhuta

Wonder


Expresses amazement, curiosity and awe.


Shanta

Peace


Represents tranquility and serenity.
